Chapter 7 Summary: Using Ability Scores

The Six Abilities
Every creature is defined by six ability scores:
• Strength — physical power
• Dexterity — agility and reflexes
• Constitution — endurance and health
• Intelligence — reasoning and memory
• Wisdom — perception and intuition
• Charisma — force of personality

A score of 10–11 is average for a human. Scores range from 1 to 30, and each has a modifier ranging from −5 to +10. To find the modifier: (score − 10) ÷ 2, rounded down.

Advantage and Disadvantage
When you have advantage, roll two d20s and take the higher. When you have disadvantage, take the lower. If you have both at the same time, they cancel out and you roll one d20 as normal. Multiple sources of advantage or disadvantage don't stack — you never roll more than two dice.

Proficiency Bonus
Determined by your level. Added to attack rolls, ability checks, and saving throws you are proficient in. It can only be added to the same roll once — never twice, even if two rules say to add it. It can be doubled (Expertise) or halved, but only once per roll.


Ability Checks
Roll a d20 + the relevant ability modifier. If the total meets or beats the DM's Difficulty Class (DC), you succeed. Common DCs:
• Very Easy: 5 | Easy: 10 | Medium: 15 | Hard: 20 | Very Hard: 25 | Nearly Impossible: 30

Skills are specific aspects of ability scores. Proficiency in a skill adds your proficiency bonus to relevant checks. Skills by ability:
• Strength: Athletics
• Dexterity: Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand, Stealth
• Intelligence: Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, Religion
• Wisdom: Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Perception, Survival
• Charisma: Deception, Intimidation, Performance, Persuasion
(Constitution has no associated skills.)

Contests - When two creatures directly oppose each other, both make ability checks and the higher total wins. On a tie, nothing changes.

Passive Checks - Represent an average, always-on result without rolling: 10 + all normal modifiers. Add 5 for advantage, subtract 5 for disadvantage. Used for things like noticing a hidden enemy without actively searching.

Working Together - One character leads with advantage on their check (reflecting help from another). The helper must be capable of doing the task alone. In combat, this uses the Help action.

Group Checks - Everyone rolls. If at least half succeed, the whole group succeeds.

The Six Abilities in Detail

Strength — Lifting, pushing, breaking, climbing, jumping, swimming. Used for melee weapon attack and damage rolls. Carrying capacity = Strength score × 15 lbs. You can push/drag/lift up to twice that (speed drops to 5 ft beyond carrying capacity).

Dexterity — Moving nimbly, picking locks, hiding, sleight of hand. Used for ranged weapon attack and damage rolls, and finesse weapon attacks. Also determines Initiative (a Dexterity check at the start of combat) and contributes to Armor Class depending on armor type.

Constitution — No associated skills. Used for holding breath, enduring exhaustion, and surviving without food or water. Your Constitution modifier is added to every Hit Die roll for hit points, and changes to your Constitution score retroactively change your HP maximum.

Intelligence — Logic, memory, recall of lore. Used by Wizards as their spellcasting ability. Relevant for recalling facts about magic, history, nature, religion, or deducing clues (Investigation).

Wisdom — Perception, intuition, reading the world. Used by Clerics, Druids, and Rangers as their spellcasting ability. Governs spotting hidden threats (Perception), tracking, animal handling, sensing deception (Insight), and first aid (Medicine).

Charisma — Influence, charm, force of personality. Used by Bards, Paladins, Sorcerers, and Warlocks as their spellcasting ability. Governs lying (Deception), threatening (Intimidation), entertaining (Performance), and friendly persuasion (Persuasion).

Saving Throws
Roll a d20 + the relevant ability modifier when forced to resist a spell, trap, poison, or similar threat. Each class grants proficiency in two saving throws, adding the proficiency bonus to those. The DC is set by whatever caused the save. A successful save usually means no harm or reduced harm.
