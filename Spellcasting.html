S p e l l L e v e l
Every spell has a level from 0 to 9. Higher level = more powerful.
• Cantrips are level 0 — cast at will, no slot needed

K n o w n a n d P r e p a r e d S p e l l s
A spellcaster must have a spell fixed in mind or accessible via a magic item before casting.
• Bards, Sorcerers: know a fixed list of spells always ready
• Clerics, Wizards: prepare spells each day from a larger list
• The number of spells you can have prepared depends on your character level
• You must be proficient with any armor you're wearing to cast spells

S p e l l S l o t s
Each spellcasting class has a limited number of spell slots per level. Casting a spell expends a slot of that spell's level or higher.
• Think of a slot as a cup — a lower-level spell fits into a higher-level slot, but not the reverse
• All expended spell slots are restored after a long rest
• Some abilities allow casting without using a slot (e.g. certain Warlock invocations)

Casting at a Higher Level - Using a higher-level slot makes the spell fill that slot's level. Some spells (e.g. magic missile, cure wounds) gain more powerful effects when upcast.

C a n t r i p s
Cast at will, no spell slot required, no preparation needed. Repeated practice fixes them permanently in the caster's mind.

R i t u a l s
Spells tagged as ritual can be cast normally or as a ritual.
• Ritual casting takes 10 extra minutes
• Does not expend a spell slot
• Cannot be cast at a higher level as a ritual
• Requires a class feature granting ritual casting (e.g. Cleric, Druid, Wizard)

C a s t i n g T i m e
Each spell specifies how long it takes to cast:
• 1 Action: most common
• Bonus Action: especially swift; you can't cast another spell the same turn except a cantrip with a 1-action casting time
• Reaction: instant; cast in response to a trigger (specified in the spell description)
• Longer times (minutes/hours): must use your action each turn and maintain concentration; if concentration breaks, the spell fails but the slot is not spent

R a n g e
The target must be within the spell's range.
• Feet: most common expression
• Touch: must physically contact the target
• Self: affects only you, or originates from you (cones, lines)
• Once cast, effects are not limited by range unless the spell says otherwise

C o m p o n e n t s
The physical requirements to cast a spell. If you can't provide a component, you can't cast the spell.

Verbal (V) - Chanting specific mystic words at the right pitch and resonance. Cannot cast if gagged or in a silence effect.

Somatic (S) - Precise hand gestures. Requires at least one free hand.

Material (M) - Specific objects listed in the spell description. Can substitute a component pouch or spellcasting focus unless the component has a cost. Consumed components must be provided fresh each casting. One hand must be free.

D u r a t i o n
How long a spell's effects last — expressed in rounds, minutes, hours, or years.
• Instantaneous: the effect happens and ends immediately; cannot be dispelled
• Concentration: must be actively maintained (see below)
• Some spells last until dispelled or destroyed

C o n c e n t r a t i o n
Some spells require concentration to stay active. You can end concentration freely (no action needed).

Things that break concentration:
• Casting another concentration spell — you can't concentrate on two spells at once
• Taking damage — make a Constitution saving throw (DC = 10 or half the damage, whichever is higher); make a separate save for each source of damage
• Being incapacitated or dying
• Certain environmental effects at the DM's discretion (DC 10 Constitution save)

T a r g e t s
A spell's description specifies what it targets.
• You must have a clear path to the target — it can't be behind total cover
• If you place an area of effect at a point you can't see and a wall is in the way, the origin appears on the near side of the obstruction
• You can target yourself with spells that target a creature of your choice, unless the spell requires a hostile creature or other than you

S a v i n g T h r o w s
Many spells allow the target to make a saving throw to resist effects.
• Spell save DC = 8 + your spellcasting ability modifier + your proficiency bonus + any special modifiers
• The spell specifies which ability is used and what happens on success or failure

A t t a c k R o l l s
Some spells require an attack roll to hit.
• Spell attack bonus = your spellcasting ability modifier + your proficiency bonus
• Most spell attacks are ranged — you have disadvantage if within 5 feet of a hostile creature that can see you and isn't incapacitated
