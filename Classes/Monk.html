Monk:

General Characteristics:
Masters of martial arts who harness the energy of ki flowing through their bodies
Study in isolated monasteries, pursuing personal perfection through contemplation and training
Channel uncanny speed and strength into their unarmed strikes
Almost always lawful in alignment due to the discipline required to master ki
Care little for material wealth; driven by a desire to accomplish a greater mission


Class Features

H i t P o i n t s
Hit Dice: 1d8 per monk level
1st Level: 8 + your Constitution modifier
Higher Levels: 1d8 + your Constitution modifier after every level

P r o f i c i e n c i e s
Armor: None
Weapons: Simple weapons, shortswords
Tools: One type of artisan's tools or one musical instrument
Saving Throws: Strength, Dexterity
Skills: Choose two from Acrobatics, Athletics, History, Insight, Religion, and Stealth
Starting Equipment:
• (a) a shortsword or (b) any simple weapon
• (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
• 10 darts

Leveling:


U n a r m o r e d D e f e n s e - While wearing no armor and not wielding a shield, your AC = 10 + Dexterity modifier + Wisdom modifier.

M a r t i a l A r t s - While unarmored or wielding only monk weapons (shortswords and simple melee weapons that aren't two-handed or heavy) and not wielding a shield:
• Use Dexterity instead of Strength for attack and damage rolls of unarmed strikes and monk weapons.
• Roll the Martial Arts die (d4 at 1st, d6 at 5th, d8 at 11th, d10 at 17th) in place of the normal damage of your unarmed strike or monk weapon.
• When you use the Attack action with an unarmed strike or monk weapon, you can make one unarmed strike as a bonus action.

K i - From 2nd level, harness mystical ki energy. Ki points equal your monk level. Regain all on a short or long rest (must spend at least 30 minutes meditating). Ki save DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + Wisdom modifier.
• Flurry of Blows - Immediately after taking the Attack action, spend 1 ki point to make two unarmed strikes as a bonus action.
• Patient Defense - Spend 1 ki point to take the Dodge action as a bonus action.
• Step of the Wind - Spend 1 ki point to take the Disengage or Dash action as a bonus action. Your jump distance is doubled for the turn.

U n a r m o r e d M o v e m e n t - From 2nd level, speed increases while unarmored and unshielded: +10 ft at 2nd, +15 ft at 6th, +20 ft at 10th, +25 ft at 14th, +30 ft at 18th. At 9th level, you can also move along vertical surfaces and across liquids without falling during the move.

M o n a s t i c T r a d i t i o n - At 3rd level, choose: Way of the Open Hand, Way of Shadow, or Way of the Four Elements. Features at 3rd, 6th, 11th, and 17th level.

D e f l e c t M i s s i l e s - From 3rd level, use your reaction when hit by a ranged weapon attack to reduce the damage by 1d10 + Dexterity modifier + monk level. If reduced to 0, you catch the missile (if small enough, one hand free). You can then spend 1 ki point to make a ranged attack with the caught weapon or ammunition as part of the same reaction (with proficiency; it counts as a monk weapon).

S l o w F a l l - From 4th level, use your reaction when falling to reduce falling damage by 5 × monk level.

E x t r a A t t a c k - From 5th level, attack twice when taking the Attack action.

S t u n n i n g S t r i k e - From 5th level, when you hit with a melee weapon attack, spend 1 ki point to attempt a stunning strike. The target must succeed on a Constitution saving throw or be stunned until the end of your next turn.

K i - E m p o w e r e d S t r i k e s - From 6th level, your unarmed strikes count as magical for overcoming resistance and immunity to nonmagical attacks and damage.

E v a s i o n - From 7th level, when subjected to an effect that allows a Dexterity saving throw for half damage, you take no damage on a success and only half on a failure.

S t i l l n e s s o f M i n d - From 7th level, use your action to end one effect on yourself causing you to be charmed or frightened.

P u r i t y o f B o d y - At 10th level, immune to disease and poison.

T o n g u e o f t h e S u n a n d M o o n - From 13th level, you understand all spoken languages, and any creature that understands a language can understand what you say.

D i a m o n d S o u l - From 14th level, proficiency in all saving throws. Also spend 1 ki point to reroll a failed saving throw (must take the second result).

T i m e l e s s B o d y - At 15th level, ki sustains you — you suffer no frailty of old age and can't be aged magically (can still die of old age). You no longer need food or water.

E m p t y B o d y - From 18th level, spend 4 ki points as an action to become invisible for 1 minute, with resistance to all damage except force. Additionally, spend 8 ki points to cast astral projection on yourself (no material components needed, no other creatures can come with you).

P e r f e c t S e l f - At 20th level, when you roll initiative with no ki points remaining, you regain 4 ki points.

W a y o f t h e O p e n H a n d:
The ultimate masters of martial arts combat, whether armed or unarmed. They learn techniques to push and trip opponents, manipulate ki to heal damage to their bodies, and practice advanced meditation that can protect them from harm.

Open Hand Technique - From 3rd level, whenever you hit a creature with one of the attacks granted by your Flurry of Blows, you can impose one of the following effects on that target:
• It must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw or be knocked prone.
• It must make a Strength saving throw. If it fails, you can push it up to 15 feet away from you.
• It can't take reactions until the end of your next turn.

Wholeness of Body - From 6th level, as an action, heal yourself for 3 × monk level HP. Once per long rest.

Tranquility - From 11th level, at the end of a long rest you gain the effect of a sanctuary spell that lasts until the start of your next long rest. Saving throw DC = 8 + Wisdom modifier + proficiency bonus. The effect can end early as normal.

Quivering Palm - From 17th level, when you hit a creature with an unarmed strike, spend 3 ki points to start imperceptible vibrations in the creature's body that last for a number of days equal to your monk level. The vibrations are harmless unless you use your action to end them (you and the target must be on the same plane). The creature must make a Constitution saving throw. On a failure, it is reduced to 0 hit points. On a success, it takes 10d10 necrotic damage. You can have only one creature under this effect at a time. You can choose to end the vibrations harmlessly without using an action.

W a y o f S h a d o w:
Monks of the Way of Shadow follow a tradition that values stealth and subterfuge — sometimes called ninjas or shadow dancers, serving as spies and assassins. They expect unquestioning obedience and operate in strict secrecy.

Shadow Arts - From 3rd level, spend 2 ki points as an action to cast darkness, darkvision, pass without trace, or silence (no material components). You also gain the minor illusion cantrip if you don't already know it.

Shadow Step - From 6th level, while in dim light or darkness, as a bonus action teleport up to 60 feet to an unoccupied space you can see that is also in dim light or darkness. You then have advantage on the first melee attack you make before the end of the turn.

Cloak of Shadows - From 11th level, while in an area of dim light or darkness, use your action to become invisible. You remain invisible until you make an attack, cast a spell, or are in an area of bright light.

Opportunist - From 17th level, when a creature within 5 feet of you is hit by an attack made by a creature other than you, use your reaction to make a melee attack against that creature.

W a y o f t h e F o u r E l e m e n t s:
You follow a monastic tradition that teaches you to harness the elements. When you focus your ki, you can align yourself with the forces of creation and bend the four elements to your will, using them as an extension of your body. Some members dedicate themselves to a single element; others weave the elements together. Many monks of this tradition tattoo their bodies with representations of their ki powers — coiling dragons, phoenixes, fish, plants, mountains, and cresting waves.

Disciple of the Elements - From 3rd level, you learn magical disciplines that harness the power of the four elements. A discipline requires you to spend ki points each time you use it. You know Elemental Attunement automatically, plus one other elemental discipline of your choice. You learn one additional discipline at 6th, 11th, and 17th level. Whenever you learn a new discipline, you can also replace one you already know with a different one.

Casting Elemental Spells - Some disciplines let you cast spells. You use the spell's normal casting time and rules but don't need material components. From 5th level, you can spend extra ki points (1 per spell level) to upcast a discipline spell, provided it has an enhanced effect at a higher level. Maximum ki points per spell cast:
• 5th–8th level monk: 3 ki points max
• 9th–12th level monk: 4 ki points max
• 13th–16th level monk: 5 ki points max
• 17th–20th level monk: 6 ki points max

— E L E M E N T A L D I S C I P L I N E S —
(Disciplines with a level requirement cannot be learned until you reach that monk level.)

Elemental Attunement (No level requirement — automatically known)
As an action, briefly control elemental forces nearby, causing one of the following effects of your choice:
• Create a harmless, instantaneous sensory effect related to air, earth, fire, or water (a shower of sparks, a puff of wind, a spray of light mist, or a gentle rumbling of stone).
• Instantaneously light or snuff out a candle, a torch, or a small campfire.
• Chill or warm up to 1 pound of nonliving material for up to 1 hour.
• Cause earth, fire, water, or mist that fits within a 1-foot cube to shape itself into a crude form you designate for 1 minute.

Fangs of the Fire Snake (No level requirement — 1 ki point)
When you use the Attack action, spend 1 ki point to cause tendrils of flame to stretch from your fists and feet. Your reach with unarmed strikes increases by 10 feet for that action and the rest of the turn. Hits with such attacks deal fire damage instead of bludgeoning. If you spend an additional 1 ki point when the attack hits, it also deals an extra 1d10 fire damage.

Fist of Four Thunders (No level requirement — 2 ki points)
Cast thunderwave.

Fist of Unbroken Air (No level requirement — 2 ki points)
As an action, choose a creature within 30 feet. It must make a Strength saving throw. On a failure, it takes 3d10 bludgeoning damage (+ 1d10 per additional ki point spent), and you can push it up to 20 feet away and knock it prone. On a success, it takes half damage and you don't push or knock it prone.

Rush of the Gale Spirits (No level requirement — 2 ki points)
Cast gust of wind.

Shape the Flowing River (No level requirement — 1 ki point)
As an action, choose an area of ice or water no larger than 30 feet on a side within 120 feet. You can change water to ice or vice versa, and reshape ice in the area in any manner you choose — raise or lower elevation, create or fill a trench, erect or flatten a wall, or form a pillar. The extent of changes can't exceed half the area's largest dimension. You can't shape the ice to trap or injure a creature in the area.

Sweeping Cinder Strike (No level requirement — 2 ki points)
Cast burning hands.

Water Whip (No level requirement — 2 ki points)
As a bonus action, create a whip of water to shove and pull a creature within 30 feet. It must make a Dexterity saving throw. On a failure, it takes 3d10 bludgeoning damage (+ 1d10 per additional ki point spent), and you can either knock it prone or pull it up to 25 feet closer to you. On a success, it takes half damage and you don't pull or knock it prone.

Clench of the North Wind (6th level required — 3 ki points)
Cast hold person.

Gong of the Summit (6th level required — 3 ki points)
Cast shatter.

Eternal Mountain Defense (11th level required — 5 ki points)
Cast stoneskin, targeting yourself.

Flames of the Phoenix (11th level required — 4 ki points)
Cast fireball.

Mist Stance (11th level required — 4 ki points)
Cast gaseous form, targeting yourself.

Ride the Wind (11th level required — 4 ki points)
Cast fly, targeting yourself.

Breath of Winter (17th level required — 6 ki points)
Cast cone of cold.

River of Hungry Flame (17th level required — 5 ki points)
Cast wall of fire.

Wave of Rolling Earth (17th level required — 6 ki points)
Cast wall of stone.
