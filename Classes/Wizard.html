Wizard:

General Characteristics:
Supreme magic-users defined and united by the spells they cast
Draw on the subtle weave of magic that permeates the cosmos
Live and die by their spells — everything else is secondary
Learn new spells through experimentation, ancient tomes, and other wizards
Choose an Arcane Tradition (school of magic) to specialize in


Class Features

H i t P o i n t s
Hit Dice: 1d6 per wizard level
1st Level: 6 + your Constitution modifier
Higher Levels: 1d6 + your Constitution modifier after every level

P r o f i c i e n c i e s
Armor: None
Weapons: Daggers, darts, slings, quarterstaffs, light crossbows
Saving Throws: Intelligence, Wisdom
Skills: Choose two from Arcana, History, Insight, Investigation, Medicine, and Religion
Starting Equipment:
• (a) a quarterstaff or (b) a dagger
• (a) a component pouch or (b) an arcane focus
• (a) a scholar's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
• A spellbook

S p e l l c a s t i n g - Intelligence is your spellcasting ability. You have a spellbook containing 6 1st-level spells at 1st level. Add 2 wizard spells to your spellbook each level. You prepare a list equal to your Intelligence modifier + wizard level. Ritual casting: cast any prepared wizard spell with the ritual tag without expending a spell slot (if it's in your spellbook, even unprepared). Spell slots up to 9th level by 20th level. You can also copy spells found on scrolls or other spellbooks into your own (2 hours and 50 gp per spell level).

A r c a n e R e c o v e r y - Once per day when you finish a short rest, recover spell slots totaling up to half your wizard level (rounded up). No slot can be 6th level or higher.

A r c a n e T r a d i t i o n - At 2nd level, choose a school: Abjuration, Conjuration, Divination, Enchantment, Evocation, Illusion, Necromancy, or Transmutation. Features at 2nd, 6th, 10th, and 14th level. Each school halves the gold and time to copy spells of that school.

S p e l l M a s t e r y - At 18th level, choose one 1st-level and one 2nd-level wizard spell in your spellbook. Cast them at their lowest level without expending a spell slot (when prepared). Change these spells after 8 hours of study.

S i g n a t u r e S p e l l s - At 20th level, choose two 3rd-level spells from your spellbook as signature spells. Always prepared, don't count against your limit, and cast each once at 3rd level per short or long rest without expending a spell slot.

S c h o o l o f A b j u r a t i o n:
Magic that blocks, banishes, or protects. Called Abjurers.
Arcane Ward (2nd): when you cast an abjuration spell of 1st level+, create a magical ward with HP = 2 × wizard level + Intelligence modifier. Ward absorbs damage instead of you. Recharge ward by casting abjuration spells (regain HP = 2 × spell level). Recreate only once per long rest.
Projected Ward (6th): when a creature within 30 feet takes damage, use your reaction to have your Arcane Ward absorb it instead.
Improved Abjuration (10th): when casting an abjuration spell requiring an ability check (like counterspell), add your proficiency bonus.
Spell Resistance (14th): advantage on saving throws against spells. Resistance to spell damage.

S c h o o l o f C o n j u r a t i o n:
Produce objects and creatures out of thin air; teleport across vast distances.
Minor Conjuration (2nd): as an action, conjure an inanimate nonmagical object up to 3 feet on a side and 10 lbs within 10 feet. Lasts 1 hour, until you use it again, or if it takes any damage.
Benign Transposition (6th): as an action, teleport up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space. Or swap places with a willing Small/Medium creature within range. Recharges when you cast a conjuration spell of 1st level+.
Focused Conjuration (10th): while concentrating on a conjuration spell, your concentration can't be broken by taking damage.
Durable Summons (14th): creatures you summon or create with conjuration spells have 30 temporary HP.

S c h o o l o f D i v i n a t i o n:
Part the veils of space, time, and consciousness to see clearly.
Portent (2nd): after a long rest, roll two d20s and record the results. Use them to replace any attack roll, saving throw, or ability check before the roll (once per turn). Lost on next long rest.
Expert Divination (6th): when casting a divination spell of 2nd level+, regain one expended spell slot of a lower level (max 5th).
The Third Eye (10th): as an action, gain one of: Darkvision (60 ft), Ethereal Sight (60 ft into the Ethereal Plane), Greater Comprehension (read any language), or See Invisibility (within 10 ft line of sight). Lasts until short or long rest.
Greater Portent (14th): roll three d20s for Portent instead of two.

S c h o o l o f E n c h a n t m e n t:
Magically entrance and beguile other people and monsters.
Hypnotic Gaze (2nd): as an action, one creature within 5 feet must make a Wisdom save or be charmed (speed 0, incapacitated, dazed). Maintain as an action each turn. Once the effect ends, can't use on that creature again until long rest.
Instinctive Charm (6th): when a creature you see within 30 feet makes an attack roll against you, use your reaction to divert the attack to the nearest other creature. Attacker makes a Wisdom save.
Split Enchantment (10th): when casting a single-target enchantment spell of 1st level+, target a second creature in range.
Alter Memories (14th): when you charm a creature with an enchantment spell, make it unaware of being charmed. Also, before the spell expires, use your action to make it forget hours of time (Intelligence save, 1 + Charisma modifier hours lost on failure).

S c h o o l o f E v o c a t i o n:
Creates powerful elemental effects — fire, cold, lightning, thunder, acid.
Sculpt Spells (2nd): when casting an evocation spell, choose up to 1 + spell level creatures that automatically succeed saves and take no damage (even on half-damage saves).
Potent Cantrip (6th): when a creature succeeds on a save against your damaging cantrip, it takes half damage (but no other effects).
Empowered Evocation (10th): add your Intelligence modifier to the damage roll of any wizard evocation spell.
Overchannel (14th): when casting a wizard spell of 5th level or lower, deal maximum damage. First use: no downside. Each subsequent use before a long rest: take 2d12 necrotic damage per spell level (increases by 1d12 per additional use).

S c h o o l o f I l l u s i o n:
Dazzle the senses and bewilder the mind — make the impossible seem real.
Improved Minor Illusion (2nd): learn the minor illusion cantrip (or another if you know it). Cast minor illusion to create both a sound and image with a single casting.
Malleable Illusions (6th): while concentrating on an illusion spell lasting 1 minute+, use your action to change the nature of the illusion (within the spell's normal parameters).
Illusory Self (10th): when a creature makes an attack roll against you, use your reaction to interpose an illusory duplicate — the attack automatically misses, then the illusion dissipates. Recharges on short or long rest.
Illusory Reality (14th): when you cast an illusion spell of 1st level+, choose one inanimate nonmagical object that is part of it and make it real for 1 minute (bonus action). It can't deal damage or directly harm anyone.

S c h o o l o f N e c r o m a n c y:
Explores the cosmic forces of life, death, and undeath.
Grim Harvest (2nd): once per turn, when you kill one or more creatures with a spell of 1st level+, regain HP equal to twice the spell's level (three times if the spell is necromancy). Doesn't apply to constructs or undead.
Undead Thralls (6th): add animate dead to your spellbook. When you cast it, target one additional corpse. Your undead get +wizard level max HP and add your proficiency bonus to weapon damage rolls.
Inured to Undeath (10th): resistance to necrotic damage; your HP maximum can't be reduced.
Command Undead (14th): as an action, choose an undead within 60 feet you can see. It makes a Charisma save (Intelligence 8+ = advantage; Intelligence 12+ = can repeat save each hour). On failure, it's friendly to you and obeys your commands until you use this feature again.

S c h o o l o f T r a n s m u t a t i o n:
Modify energy and matter — the world is mutable and you are its agent of change.
Minor Alchemy (2nd): spend 10 minutes per cubic foot to temporarily transform a nonmagical object of wood, stone, iron, copper, or silver into another of those materials. Lasts 1 hour (concentration).
Transmuter's Stone (6th): over 8 hours, create a stone that grants one benefit to whoever holds it: darkvision (60 ft), +10 speed (unencumbered), Constitution save proficiency, or resistance to one damage type. Change the benefit each time you cast a transmutation spell of 1st level+.
Shapechanger (10th): add polymorph to your spellbook. Cast it without expending a spell slot (can only target yourself, into CR 1 or lower beast). Once per short or long rest.
Master Transmuter (14th): as an action, consume your transmuter's stone to choose one effect: Major Transformation (transform one nonmagical object up to a 5-foot cube into another of similar size/mass), Panacea (remove all curses, diseases, and poisons from a touched creature and restore all HP), Restore Life (cast raise dead on a touched creature without using a spell slot or needing it in your spellbook), or Restore Youth (reduce a willing creature's apparent age by 3d10 years, min 13).
