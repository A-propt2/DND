Rogue:

General Characteristics:
Scoundrels who use stealth and trickery to overcome obstacles and enemies
Devote as much effort to mastering skills as to perfecting combat abilities
Prioritize cunning over brute strength — one precise strike over many blows
Have an almost supernatural knack for avoiding danger
Found on both sides of the law — from criminals to professional treasure hunters


Class Features

H i t P o i n t s
Hit Dice: 1d8 per rogue level
1st Level: 8 + your Constitution modifier
Higher Levels: 1d8 + your Constitution modifier after every level

P r o f i c i e n c i e s
Armor: Light armor
Weapons: Simple weapons, hand crossbows, longswords, rapiers, shortswords
Tools: Thieves' tools
Saving Throws: Dexterity, Intelligence
Skills: Choose four from Acrobatics, Athletics, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Investigation, Perception, Performance, Persuasion, Sleight of Hand, and Stealth
Starting Equipment:
• (a) a rapier or (b) a shortsword
• (a) a shortbow and 20 arrows or (b) a shortsword
• (a) a burglar's pack, (b) a dungeoneer's pack, or (c) an explorer's pack
• Leather armor, two daggers, and thieves' tools

Leveling: See Rogue table for Sneak Attack damage scaling (1d6 at 1st to 10d6 at 20th).

E x p e r t i s e - At 1st level, choose two skill proficiencies or one skill and thieves' tools — proficiency bonus doubled for those. Choose two more at 6th level.

S n e a k A t t a c k - Once per turn, deal extra damage (see table) to one creature you hit with a finesse or ranged weapon if you have advantage on the roll, or if an ally is within 5 feet of the target (and you don't have disadvantage).

T h i e v e s ' C a n t - During training, you learned Thieves' Cant — a secret mix of dialect, jargon, and code to hide messages in seemingly normal conversation. Only other Thieves' Cant speakers understand these messages.

C u n n i n g A c t i o n - From 2nd level, as a bonus action on each turn in combat, take the Dash, Disengage, or Hide action.

R o g u i s h A r c h e t y p e - At 3rd level, choose: Thief, Assassin, or Arcane Trickster. Features at 3rd, 9th, 13th, and 17th level.

U n c a n n y D o d g e - From 5th level, when an attacker you can see hits you, use your reaction to halve the damage.

E v a s i o n - From 7th level, you can dodge some AoE attacks and when subjected to a Dexterity save for half damage, take no damage on success, and half on failure.

R e l i a b l e T a l e n t - From 11th level, when making an ability check that uses proficiency, treat any d20 roll of 9 or lower as a 10.

B l i n d s e n s e - From 14th level, if you can hear, you're aware of the location of any hidden or invisible creature within 10 feet.

S l i p p e r y M i n d - From 15th level, gain proficiency in Wisdom saving throws.

E l u s i v e - From 18th level, no attack roll has advantage against you while you aren't incapacitated.

S t r o k e o f L u c k - At 20th level, if your attack misses, turn it into a hit. Or if you fail an ability check, treat the d20 roll as a 20. Once per short or long rest.

T h i e f:
Expert in larceny — burglars, treasure seekers, and delvers of ancient ruins.
Fast Hands (3rd): use the bonus action from Cunning Action to make a Sleight of Hand check, use thieves' tools, or take the Use an Object action.
Second-Story Work (3rd): climbing costs no extra movement. Running jump distance increases by Dexterity modifier.
Supreme Sneak (9th): advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks if you move no more than half speed.
Use Magic Device (13th): ignore all class, race, and level requirements on magic items.
Thief's Reflexes (17th): take two turns in the first round of combat — one at normal initiative, one at initiative minus 10. Can't use if surprised.

A s s a s s i n:
Masters of death — hired killers, spies, bounty hunters. Use stealth and poison.
Bonus Proficiencies (3rd): proficiency with the disguise kit and poisoner's kit.
Assassinate (3rd): advantage on attacks against creatures that haven't taken a turn yet. Hits against surprised creatures are critical hits.
Infiltration Expertise (9th): spend 7 days and 25 gp to create an undetectable false identity.
Impostor (13th): unerringly mimic another person's speech, writing, and behavior after 3 hours of study. Advantage on Deception checks to avoid detection.
Death Strike (17th): when you hit a surprised creature, it must make a Constitution save (DC 8 + Dex modifier + proficiency) or take double damage.

A r c a n e T r i c k s t e r:

Enhances stealth and agility with enchantment and illusion magic from the wizard list.
Spellcasting (3rd): Intelligence is your spellcasting ability. Learn mage hand plus 2 wizard cantrips. Gain spell slots up to 4th level by 20th level. Spells primarily from enchantment and illusion schools.
Mage Hand Legerdemain (3rd): make your mage hand invisible; use it to stow/retrieve objects in others' containers or pick locks/disarm traps at range.
Magical Ambush (9th): if you're hidden when you cast a spell on a creature, it has disadvantage on any saving throw against that spell.
Versatile Trickster (13th): as a bonus action, use your mage hand to distract a creature — you have advantage on attacks against it until end of turn.
Spell Thief (17th): after a creature casts a spell targeting you, use your reaction to force a spellcasting ability save. On failure, you steal the knowledge of that spell for 8 hours and negate its effects against you.
