Druid:

General Characteristics:
Priests of the Old Faith who wield the powers of nature
Embody nature's resilience, cunning, and fury
See themselves as extensions of nature's indomitable will, not its masters
Oppose cults of Elemental Evil and unnatural creatures like undead and aberrations
Will not wear armor or use shields made of metal


Class Features

H i t P o i n t s
Hit Dice: 1d8 per druid level
1st Level: 8 + your Constitution modifier
Higher Levels: 1d8 + your Constitution modifier after every level

P r o f i c i e n c i e s
Armor: Light armor, medium armor, shields (no metal)
Weapons: Clubs, daggers, darts, javelins, maces, quarterstaffs, scimitars, sickles, slings, spears
Tools: Herbalism kit
Saving Throws: Intelligence, Wisdom
Skills: Choose two from Arcana, Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Nature, Perception, Religion, and Survival
Starting Equipment:
• (a) a wooden shield or (b) any simple weapon
• (a) a scimitar or (b) any simple melee weapon
• Leather armor, an explorer's pack, and a druidic focus

D r u i d i c - You know Druidic, the secret language of druids. You can use it to leave hidden messages that only other druids automatically spot.

S p e l l c a s t i n g - Wisdom is your spellcasting ability. You know 2 cantrips at 1st level. You prepare spells equal to your Wisdom modifier + druid level. You can cast prepared druid spells as rituals if they have the ritual tag.

W i l d S h a p e - From 2nd level, use your action to magically assume the shape of a beast you have seen. Use twice, regain on short or long rest. Duration: half your druid level in hours (rounded down).
Beast shape limits by level:
• 2nd level: CR 1/4, no flying or swimming speed (e.g. Wolf)
• 4th level: CR 1/2, no flying speed (e.g. Crocodile)
• 8th level: CR 1, any beast (e.g. Giant Eagle)
While transformed: use the beast's stats but keep your alignment, personality, and mental scores. Can't cast spells (except with Beast Spells at 18th level).

D r u i d C i r c l e - At 2nd level, choose a circle: Circle of the Land or Circle of the Moon. Grants features at 2nd, 6th, 10th, and 14th level.

T i m e l e s s B o d y - From 18th level, for every 10 years that pass, your body ages only 1 year.

B e a s t S p e l l s - From 18th level, you can cast druid spells in beast form (somatic and verbal components only).

A r c h d r u i d - At 20th level, you can use Wild Shape an unlimited number of times. Also ignore verbal and somatic components and non-consumed material components for druid spells.

C i r c l e o f t h e L a n d:
Mystics who safeguard ancient knowledge.
Bonus Cantrip - Learn one additional druid cantrip at 2nd level.
Natural Recovery - Once per day during a short rest, recover spell slots totaling up to half your druid level (rounded up, max 5th level slots).
Circle Spells - At 3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th level gain spells based on terrain. Always prepared.


Land's Stride - From 6th: move through nonmagical difficult terrain without cost. Advantage on saves against magically created plants.
Nature's Ward - At 10th: can't be charmed or frightened by elementals or fey; immune to poison and disease.
Nature's Sanctuary - At 14th: beasts and plant creatures must make a Wisdom save or choose a different target when attacking you.

C i r c l e o f t h e M o o n:
Fierce guardians of the wilds who prefer beast form.
Combat Wild Shape - From 2nd: use Wild Shape as a bonus action, and spend spell slots while transformed to regain 1d8 HP per slot level.
Circle Forms - From 2nd: transform into beasts up to CR 1 (ignoring the table). From 6th: up to CR = druid level ÷ 3.
Elemental Wild Shape - At 10th: spend two Wild Shape uses to transform into an air, earth, fire, or water elemental.
Thousand Forms - At 14th: cast alter self at will.
