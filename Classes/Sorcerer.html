Sorcerer:

General Characteristics:
Spellcasters who draw on inherent magic from a gift or bloodline
Carry a magical birthright — one can't study sorcery, the power chooses the sorcerer
Have no use for spellbooks — they channel their own inborn magic
Magic can spill out unpredictably if not wielded; it wants to be used
Make up for narrower spell knowledge with enormous flexibility using Metamagic


Class Features

H i t P o i n t s
Hit Dice: 1d6 per sorcerer level
1st Level: 6 + your Constitution modifier
Higher Levels: 1d6 + your Constitution modifier after every level

P r o f i c i e n c i e s
Armor: None
Weapons: Daggers, darts, slings, quarterstaffs, light crossbows
Saving Throws: Constitution, Charisma
Skills: Choose two from Arcana, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Persuasion, and Religion
Starting Equipment:
• (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) any simple weapon
• (a) a component pouch or (b) an arcane focus
• (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
• Two daggers

S p e l l c a s t i n g - Charisma is your spellcasting ability. Know 4 cantrips at 1st level and 2 spells. Gain more as shown in the Sorcerer table. You can replace one known spell per level up. Spell slots up to 9th level by 20th level.

S o r c e r o u s O r i g i n - At 1st level, choose: Draconic Bloodline or Wild Magic. Features at 1st, 6th, 14th, and 18th level.

F o n t o f M a g i c - From 2nd level, you have sorcery points equal to your sorcerer level. Regain all on long rest.
• Creating Spell Slots: spend sorcery points as a bonus action to gain a spell slot (2 for 1st level, 3 for 2nd, 5 for 3rd, 6 for 4th, 7 for 5th level slots — max 5th).
• Converting Spell Slots: as a bonus action, expend a spell slot to gain sorcery points equal to its level.

M e t a m a g i c - At 3rd level, gain 2 Metamagic options. Gain one more at 10th and 17th level. Can only use one per spell cast (unless noted).
• Careful Spell - Spend 1 point: up to Charisma modifier creatures automatically succeed saves against the spell.
• Distant Spell - Spend 1 point: double range (or change touch to 30 ft).
• Empowered Spell - Spend 1 point: reroll up to Charisma modifier damage dice (must use new rolls). Can stack with one other Metamagic.
• Extended Spell - Spend 1 point: double duration (max 24 hours).
• Heightened Spell - Spend 3 points: one target has disadvantage on its first save against the spell.
• Quickened Spell - Spend 2 points: change casting time from 1 action to 1 bonus action.
• Subtle Spell - Spend 1 point: cast without somatic or verbal components.
• Twinned Spell - Spend spell level in points (min 1): target a second creature with a single-target spell.

S o r c e r o u s R e s t o r a t i o n - At 20th level, regain 4 sorcery points whenever you finish a short rest.

D r a c o n i c B l o o d l i n e:
Magic comes from draconic ancestry — your blood is infused with the magic of dragons.
Dragon Ancestor (1st): choose a dragon type and its damage type (Black=Acid, Blue=Lightning, Brass=Fire, Bronze=Lightning, Copper=Acid, Gold=Fire, Green=Poison, Red=Fire, Silver=Cold, White=Cold). Speak, read, and write Draconic. Doubled proficiency bonus on Charisma checks with dragons.
Draconic Resilience (1st): HP maximum increases by 1 per sorcerer level. While unarmored, AC = 13 + Dexterity modifier.
Elemental Affinity (6th): add Charisma modifier to damage of your ancestor's type. Spend 1 sorcery point for resistance to that type for 1 hour.
Dragon Wings (14th): as a bonus action, sprout dragon wings — flying speed equal to your speed.
Draconic Presence (18th): spend 5 sorcery points to emit a 60-foot aura of awe or fear for 1 minute. Enemies that start their turn in it make a Wisdom save or are charmed/frightened.

W i l d M a g i c:
Magic comes from the chaotic forces underlying creation — unpredictable and explosive.
Wild Magic Surge (1st): after casting a sorcerer spell of 1st level or higher, the DM can have you roll d20. On a 1, roll on the Wild Magic Surge table for a random magical effect.
Tides of Chaos (1st): gain advantage on one attack roll, ability check, or saving throw. Must finish a long rest before using again (though the DM may trigger a Wild Magic Surge first which lets you use it again).
Bend Luck (6th): spend 2 sorcery points as a reaction to roll 1d4 and add or subtract it from another creature's roll (after you see it, before effects apply).
Controlled Chaos (14th): when rolling on the Wild Magic Surge table, roll twice and choose either result.
Spell Bombardment (18th): once per turn, when you roll the highest number on a damage die for a spell, roll that die again and add it to the damage.
