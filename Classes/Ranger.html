Ranger:

General Characteristics:
Warriors of the wilderness who specialize in hunting monsters that threaten civilization
Track their quarry like predators, moving stealthily through the wilds
Learn to cast spells that harness nature's power, much as a druid does
Fiercely independent — often the first and last line of defense in frontier lands
Comfortable far from the comforts of cities, acclimated to hardship


Class Features

H i t P o i n t s
Hit Dice: 1d10 per ranger level
1st Level: 10 + your Constitution modifier
Higher Levels: 1d10 + your Constitution modifier after every level

P r o f i c i e n c i e s
Armor: Light armor, medium armor, shields
Weapons: Simple weapons, martial weapons
Saving Throws: Strength, Dexterity
Skills: Choose three from Animal Handling, Athletics, Insight, Investigation, Nature, Perception, Stealth, and Survival
Starting Equipment:
• (a) scale mail or (b) leather armor
• (a) two shortswords or (b) two simple melee weapons
• (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
• A longbow and a quiver of 20 arrows

F a v o r e d E n e m y - From 1st level, choose a type of enemy (aberrations, beasts, celestials, constructs, dragons, elementals, fey, fiends, giants, monstrosities, oozes, plants, or undead — or two humanoid races). You have advantage on Wisdom (Survival) checks to track them and Intelligence checks to recall information about them. You also learn one language they speak. Choose one more at 6th and 14th level.

N a t u r a l E x p l o r e r - From 1st level, choose a favored terrain type (arctic, coast, desert, forest, grassland, mountain, swamp, or Underdark). Intelligence/Wisdom checks related to it: proficiency bonus doubled. While traveling in it: difficult terrain doesn't slow the group, group can't get lost by non-magical means, you remain alert while doing other activities, you move stealthily at normal pace if alone, you forage twice as much food, and you learn exact number/size/age of animal tracks. Choose more terrain types at 6th and 10th level.

F i g h t i n g S t y l e - At 2nd level, choose: Archery (+2 to ranged attack rolls), Defense (+1 AC), Dueling (+2 damage one-handed), or Two-Weapon Fighting (add ability modifier to second attack damage).

S p e l l c a s t i n g - From 2nd level, Wisdom is your spellcasting ability. Know 2 spells at 2nd level; gain more as shown in the Ranger table. Spell slots up to 5th level by 20th level. Can replace one known spell per level up.

R a n g e r A r c h e t y p e - At 3rd level, choose: Hunter or Beast Master. Features at 3rd, 7th, 11th, and 15th level.

P r i m e v a l A w a r e n e s s - From 3rd level, expend a spell slot as an action to sense whether aberrations, celestials, dragons, elementals, fey, fiends, or undead are within 1 mile (6 miles in favored terrain) for 1 minute per spell level. Doesn't reveal location or number.

E x t r a A t t a c k - From 5th level, attack twice when taking the Attack action.

L a n d ' s S t r i d e - From 8th level, nonmagical difficult terrain costs no extra movement. Pass through nonmagical plants without damage. Advantage on saves against magically created plants.

H i d e i n P l a i n S i g h t - From 10th level, spend 1 minute camouflaging yourself with natural materials. Then gain +10 to Dexterity (Stealth) checks while pressed against a solid surface without moving.

V a n i s h - From 14th level, use the Hide action as a bonus action. Can't be tracked by nonmagical means (unless you choose to leave a trail).

F e r a l S e n s e s - At 18th level, attacking a creature you can't see doesn't impose disadvantage. You're also aware of any invisible creature within 30 feet (if not hidden from you and you're not blinded/deafened).

F o e S l a y e r - At 20th level, once per turn, add your Wisdom modifier to an attack roll or damage roll against a favored enemy.

H u n t e r:
A bulwark between civilization and the terrors of the wilderness.
Hunter's Prey (3rd): choose one — Colossus Slayer (extra 1d8 damage to enemies who are below max hp once per turn), Giant Killer (can use your reaction to attack against creatures larger than you that hit or miss you), or Horde Breaker (attack a second adjacent creature with the same weapon once per turn).
Defensive Tactics (7th): choose one — Escape the Horde (opportunity attacks against you have disadvantage), Multiattack Defense (+4 AC against all subsequent attacks by one attacker), or Steel Will (advantage on saves against fright).
Multiattack (11th): choose one — Volley (ranged attack against any number of creatures within a 10-ft radius) or Whirlwind Attack (melee attack against any number of creatures within 5 feet).
Superior Hunter's Defense (15th): choose one — Evasion (can dodge some AoE attacks and take no damage on a Dex save success, half on fail), Stand Against the Tide (redirect a hostile creature's missed attack to another creature), or Uncanny Dodge (halve one attack's damage as a reaction).

B e a s t M a s t e r:
United in focus with an animal companion.
Ranger's Companion (3rd): gain a Medium or smaller beast companion (CR 1/4 or lower). Add proficiency bonus to its AC, attacks, damage, saves, and skills. HP max = its normal max or 4 × ranger level (whichever is higher). Command it as an action to Attack, Dash, Disengage, Dodge, or Help.
Exceptional Training (7th): as a bonus action, command beast to Dash, Disengage, Dodge, or Help on turns it doesn't attack.
Bestial Fury (11th): beast can make two attacks when you command it to attack.
Share Spells (15th): when you cast a self-targeting spell, it can also affect your beast companion within 30 feet.
