C o m b a t S t e p b y S t e p
1. Determine surprise – DM checks if either side is surprised
2. Establish positions – DM places all characters and monsters
3. Roll initiative – Everyone rolls, setting the turn order
4. Take turns – Each combatant acts in initiative order
5. Begin the next round – Repeat until one side is defeated

S u r p r i s e
The DM compares Dexterity (Stealth) checks of anyone hiding against the passive Wisdom (Perception) of each creature on the opposing side.
• A surprised creature cannot move or take actions on its first turn, and cannot take reactions until that turn ends.
• Individual members of a group can be surprised even if others aren't.

I n i t i a t i v e
Everyone makes a Dexterity check at the start of combat. The DM ranks combatants from highest to lowest — this is the initiative order and stays the same every round.
• On a tie: players decide among themselves; DM decides for monsters.

Y o u r T u r n
On your turn you can move (up to your speed) and take one action in any order.

Bonus Actions - Some class features, spells, or abilities grant a bonus action. You can only take one bonus action per turn.

Reactions - An instant response to a trigger, usable once per round (on your turn or someone else's). The most common reaction is an opportunity attack.

Other Activity - Free on your turn: brief communication and interacting with one object (drawing a weapon, opening a door, etc.). Interacting with a second object requires your action.

M o v e m e n t a n d P o s i t i o n
Breaking Up Movement - You can split movement before and after your action.
Difficult Terrain - Costs 1 extra foot per foot moved. Another creature's space also counts as difficult terrain.
Being Prone - Drop prone for free; standing up costs half your speed. Crawling costs 1 extra foot per foot.
Moving Around Creatures - You can move through a non-hostile creature's space. Moving through a hostile creature's space requires it to be at least two sizes larger or smaller than you. You can't willingly end your move in another creature's space.
Flying Movement - Knocked prone, speed reduced to 0, or deprived of movement causes a flying creature to fall unless it can hover or is held by magic.
Squeezing - A creature can squeeze through a space one size smaller than it, but spends 1 extra foot per foot and has disadvantage on attacks and Dexterity saves. Attack rolls against it have advantage.

A c t i o n s i n C o m b a t

Attack - Make one melee or ranged attack. Some features (e.g. Extra Attack) allow more.

Cast a Spell - Most spells have a casting time of 1 action. See chapter 10 for full rules.

Dash - Gain extra movement equal to your speed for this turn.

Disengage - Your movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks for the rest of the turn.

Dodge - Until the start of your next turn, attack rolls against you have disadvantage (if you can see the attacker), and you make Dexterity saves with advantage. Lost if incapacitated or speed drops to 0.

Help - Give a creature advantage on their next ability check for a task you assist with, OR give an ally advantage on their next attack roll against a target within 5 feet of you.

Hide - Make a Dexterity (Stealth) check to hide (see chapter 7).

Ready - Declare a trigger and a response. When the trigger occurs, use your reaction to act. Readying a spell requires concentration.

Search - Make a Wisdom (Perception) or Intelligence (Investigation) check to find something.

Use an Object - For objects that require your action, or when you want to interact with more than one object.

M a k i n g a n A t t a c k
1. Choose a target within range
2. Determine modifiers – cover, advantage/disadvantage, bonuses/penalties
3. Resolve the attack – roll attack, then roll damage on a hit

Attack Rolls - Roll d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus (if proficient). Equals or exceeds target's AC = hit.
• Melee attacks use Strength; ranged attacks use Dexterity (finesse/thrown weapons can use either)
• Roll a natural 20 = automatic hit and critical hit
• Roll a natural 1 = automatic miss

Unseen Attackers and Targets
• Attacking a target you can't see: disadvantage on the attack roll
• Attacking while unseen by your target: advantage on the attack roll
• If hidden when you attack, you reveal your location whether you hit or miss

R a n g e d A t t a c k s
• Weapons with two ranges: attacks beyond normal range have disadvantage; can't attack beyond long range
• Making a ranged attack within 5 feet of a hostile creature that can see you: disadvantage

M e l e e A t t a c k s
• Standard reach is 5 feet. Larger creatures may have greater reach.
• Opportunity Attacks: When a hostile creature you can see moves out of your reach, use your reaction to make one melee attack. Avoided by taking the Disengage action.
• Two-Weapon Fighting: After attacking with a light melee weapon, use a bonus action to attack with a second light melee weapon in your other hand. No ability modifier added to the bonus attack's damage (unless negative).
• Grappling: Use Attack action; make a Strength (Athletics) check contested by target's Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics). On success, target is grappled. Moving a grappled creature halves your speed.
• Shoving: Use Attack action; same contested check. On success, either knock target prone or push it 5 feet away.

C o v e r
• Half cover: +2 bonus to AC and Dexterity saves (at least half the body blocked)
• Three-quarters cover: +5 bonus to AC and Dexterity saves (about three-quarters blocked)
• Total cover: cannot be targeted directly by attacks or spells (fully concealed)

D a m a g e a n d H e a l i n g

Damage Rolls - Roll the weapon's or spell's damage dice + ability modifier. If a spell hits multiple targets simultaneously, roll damage once for all of them.

Critical Hits - Roll all damage dice twice and add together, then add modifiers normally.

Damage Types: Acid, Bludgeoning, Cold, Fire, Force, Lightning, Necrotic, Piercing, Poison, Psychic, Radiant, Slashing, Thunder. Types have no rules of their own but interact with resistance and vulnerability.

Resistance and Vulnerability
• Resistance: damage of that type is halved
• Vulnerability: damage of that type is doubled
• Applied after all other modifiers. Multiple instances of the same type still count as only one.

Healing - Hit points regained are added to current HP, but cannot exceed your maximum. Dead creatures can't regain HP without magic (e.g. revivify).

D r o p p i n g t o 0 H i t P o i n t s

Instant Death - If remaining damage after reaching 0 HP equals or exceeds your hit point maximum, you die instantly.

Falling Unconscious - If you reach 0 HP and don't die instantly, you fall unconscious. Ends if you regain any HP.

Death Saving Throws - At the start of each of your turns at 0 HP, roll a d20:
• 10 or higher: success
• Below 10: failure
• 3 successes: you become stable
• 3 failures: you die
• Roll a natural 1: counts as two failures
• Roll a natural 20: regain 1 hit point
• Taking damage at 0 HP: suffer one failure (two if a critical hit); instant death if damage ≥ your HP maximum

Stabilizing a Creature - Use your action to make a DC 10 Wisdom (Medicine) check. A stable creature doesn't make death saves but remains unconscious. Regains 1 HP after 1d4 hours if not healed.

Knocking a Creature Out - When a melee attack reduces a creature to 0 HP, the attacker can choose to knock it out instead of kill it. The creature falls unconscious and is stable.

Temporary Hit Points - A buffer against damage; lost first before real HP. Cannot be healed or stacked — if you gain new temp HP, choose whether to keep the old or new amount. Lasts until depleted or until you finish a long rest.

M o u n t e d C o m b a t
Mount must be at least one size larger than you with appropriate anatomy.
• Mounting or dismounting costs movement equal to half your speed
• Controlled mount: initiative changes to match yours; can only Dash, Disengage, or Dodge
• Independent mount: keeps its own initiative and acts freely
• Knocked prone while mounted: DC 10 Dexterity save or fall off prone within 5 feet

U n d e r w a t e r C o m b a t
• Melee weapon attacks without a swimming speed have disadvantage unless using a dagger, javelin, shortsword, spear, or trident
• Ranged weapon attacks automatically miss beyond normal range; attacks within normal range still have disadvantage unless using a crossbow, net, or thrown weapon
• Creatures and objects fully submerged have resistance to fire damage
